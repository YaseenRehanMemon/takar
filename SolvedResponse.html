SECTION A — PROSE SHORT QUESTIONS WITH SOLUTIONS

Questions

1) Why did Quaid-e-Azam stress equality, religious freedom, and law and order in his address?

Answer

Quaid-e-Azam stressed equality, religious freedom, and law and order because he wanted Pakistan to become a just and peaceful state. He believed that all citizens, regardless of religion, caste, or background, were equal members of the nation. He wanted people to live freely and safely, and he considered law and order essential for national progress and stability.

2) Why did Quaid call bribery, corruption, black-marketing, and nepotism great evils?

Answer

Quaid called these practices great evils because they destroy justice, honesty, and merit in society. Bribery and corruption weaken institutions, black-marketing creates suffering for common people, and nepotism promotes unfairness. He wanted Pakistan to be built on honesty, discipline, and equal opportunity rather than selfish interest.

3) Why does the writer return to the lake, and what changes does he notice there?

Answer

The writer returns to the lake because it is deeply connected with his childhood memories and gives him spiritual peace. He notices that many things have changed, such as the use of motorboats and the general atmosphere of the place. However, the beauty of nature still remains, and this mixture of change and continuity creates a strong emotional effect on him.

4) Why does the lake remain a “holy spot” for the writer?

Answer

The lake remains a holy spot for the writer because it is associated with his childhood, innocence, and family memories. It gives him a sense of peace and emotional closeness to the past. For him, the lake is not just a place of beauty but a symbol of timeless love and memory.

5) Why did Mr. Loisel expect his wife to be pleased with the invitation, and how did she react?

Answer

Mr. Loisel expected his wife to be pleased because the invitation was to a grand party at the Minister’s house, and he thought she would feel honored and excited. Instead of being happy, Mathilde became upset because she had no fine dress or jewellery to wear. Her reaction showed her dissatisfaction with her social position and her love for luxury.

6) What happened after the necklace was lost, and how did it change Mathilde’s life?

Answer

After the necklace was lost, Mathilde and her husband borrowed money and bought a costly replacement. To repay the debt, they lived a life of poverty and hard labor for ten years. This changed Mathilde completely. She lost her beauty, comfort, and youthful charm. The incident taught her a harsh lesson about vanity and false pride.

7) Why were the sacrifices of the Loisels ultimately useless?

Answer

The sacrifices of the Loisels were ultimately useless because the original necklace was fake and inexpensive. They suffered for ten years to replace something that had little real value. Their suffering became meaningless because it was based on appearance, vanity, and misunderstanding. The story exposes the irony of their fate.

8) What is meant by technological revolution, and how has it changed modern life?

Answer

Technological revolution means the rapid development and use of modern technology in different fields of life. It has changed modern life by improving communication, education, health care, business, entertainment, and security. It has made life faster, easier, and more efficient, and it has connected people across the world.

9) How does technology help in education, medicine, and disaster management?

Answer

Technology helps education through online classes, digital learning tools, and easy access to information. In medicine, it supports diagnosis, telemedicine, and better treatment methods. In disaster management, it helps in forecasting, monitoring, rescue operations, and communication during emergencies. Thus, technology has become highly useful in protecting and improving human life.

10) What effect did entering the bank have on the writer, and why?

Answer

Entering the bank made the writer extremely nervous and confused. He felt uncomfortable because he was not familiar with banking matters and became overly self-conscious. His fear caused him to behave awkwardly, which created the humorous situation in the story.

11) Why did the bank manager misunderstand the writer?

Answer

The bank manager misunderstood the writer because the writer behaved in a strange, secretive, and nervous manner. When he requested to see the manager alone, the manager thought he was an important or suspicious person. In reality, the writer was simply an ordinary man trying to open a small bank account.

12) What makes a man great according to Emerson, and why is self-reliance important?

Answer

According to Emerson, a man becomes great when he trusts himself and follows his own conscience. Self-reliance is important because it gives a person confidence, independence, and courage. It teaches one not to imitate others but to believe in one’s own abilities and principles. Emerson believes that true peace and success come from inner strength.

13) Why was Booker T. Washington inspired to go to Hampton, and what difficulties did he face?

Answer

Booker T. Washington was inspired to go to Hampton because he had a deep desire for education and had heard that Hampton was an excellent institution. On his journey, he faced many difficulties including poverty, hunger, exhaustion, and lack of shelter. He even had to sleep under a sidewalk. His struggle shows his determination and love for learning.

14) Why is Singapore called “Mr. Clean of the world”?

Answer

Singapore is called “Mr. Clean of the world” because it is extremely neat, orderly, and well-maintained. The government enforces strict cleanliness rules, and the people follow discipline in public life. Clean roads, proper waste management, and heavy fines for littering make Singapore one of the cleanest cities in the world.

15) Differentiate between a job and a career. What should one keep in mind before choosing a career?

Answer

A job is a means of earning money, while a career is a long-term professional path based on interest, skill, and growth. Before choosing a career, a person should consider personal interests, abilities, values, aptitude, personality, and future opportunities. A wise career choice leads to satisfaction and success.

SECTION A — POETRY SHORT QUESTIONS WITH SOLUTIONS

Questions

1) Explain the stanza from “The Abbot of Canterbury” with reference to context.

Answer

This stanza occurs when the Abbot seeks time before appearing before King John. It shows his worry and helplessness because he knows the king has asked difficult questions. At the same time, it prepares the reader for the clever plan that follows. The tone is narrative and interesting, and it builds suspense in the poem.

2) What were the three questions asked by King John, and what answers were given by the shepherd?

Answer

King John asked three questions: how much he was worth, how long it would take him to go around the world, and what he was thinking at that moment. The shepherd answered that the king was worth one penny less than Christ, that he could go around the world in twenty-four hours if he travelled with the sun, and that the king thought he was speaking to the Abbot when he was actually speaking to the shepherd. These answers show intelligence and wit.

3) What did Lucy Gray reply to her father, and what happened to her afterwards?

Answer

Lucy Gray replied obediently and agreed to go to town with the lantern. On her way, she was caught in a snowstorm and disappeared. Her parents searched for her footprints, but they ended at the bridge, and she was never found. The poem ends with a mysterious and sorrowful note.

4) How is Khahori’s character depicted by the poet?

Answer

Khahori is depicted as a wandering ascetic who is patient, disciplined, devoted, and spiritually strong. He faces hardship, loneliness, and suffering in search of truth. The poet presents him as a symbol of spiritual struggle and deep love for the Beloved.

5) What is the main theme of “Don’t Quit”?

Answer

The main theme of the poem is perseverance. It teaches that difficulties and failure should not discourage a person. One must continue trying because success may be very near. The poem inspires hope, patience, and determination.

6) What qualities make a man’s life happy in “The Character of a Happy Life”?

Answer

A happy man is honest, self-controlled, contented, and free from envy and evil desires. He does not become a slave to others’ wishes and lives according to moral values. The poem shows that true happiness comes from character and inner peace, not from wealth.

7) What message does the poet convey in “Good Timber”?

Answer

The poet conveys that hardship builds strength and character. Just as strong trees grow in difficult conditions, strong people are shaped by struggle and effort. The poem teaches that comfort does not create greatness; difficulty does.

8) What irony is presented in “Ozymandias”?

Answer

The irony in the poem is that Ozymandias once considered himself a mighty and unforgettable ruler, but now his statue lies broken in a lonely desert. His power has disappeared, while the ruins silently mock his pride. The poem shows that worldly power is temporary.

9) How does the sculptor appear greater than Ozymandias himself?

Answer

The sculptor appears greater because he successfully captured the ruler’s pride and cruel expression in stone. Although Ozymandias’s kingdom vanished, the sculptor’s art survived and continues to reveal the king’s personality. In this way, art proves more lasting than political power.

10) What reward comes after hardship in “Sur Khahori”?

Answer

The reward after hardship in “Sur Khahori” is spiritual realization and closeness to the Beloved. The poem suggests that truth and inner peace can only be reached through patience, sacrifice, and struggle. The journey is difficult, but the result is spiritually valuable.

SECTION A — DRAMA SHORT QUESTIONS WITH SOLUTIONS

Questions

1) What restrictions were imposed on Spelding’s family?

Answer

Spelding’s family was placed under strict control after Kreton’s arrival created confusion and fear. Their movement and communication were limited because the authorities wanted to manage the strange situation. These restrictions show the panic and suspicion of the officials.

2) How is Kreton able to communicate with the people on earth?

Answer

Kreton is able to communicate with earth people through his superior mental powers. He can read thoughts and understand others without normal language barriers. This ability shows that his race is more advanced than human beings.

3) Describe the character of Ellen in a nutshell.

Answer

Ellen is young, lively, curious, and emotionally open. She is more imaginative and less fearful than others in the play. She is attracted to the unusual personality of Kreton and responds to him with interest rather than panic. Her character adds freshness and sensitivity to the drama.

4) Kreton’s people are more advanced and civilized than the people on earth. Discuss.

Answer

Kreton’s people are shown as more advanced because they possess greater knowledge, mental powers, and technological superiority. Kreton can read minds and control situations easily. However, the drama also suggests that advancement without emotional understanding may not be complete civilization. So the comparison is both comic and thought-provoking.

5) Why does Kreton call earth a small planet?

Answer

Kreton calls earth a small planet because he comes from a much more advanced world and sees earth from a broader cosmic point of view. The phrase also reflects his pride and the play’s satire on human importance. It reminds the audience that man is not as great as he imagines himself to be.

6) Why does Kreton come to earth, and what is his attitude toward human beings?

Answer

Kreton comes to earth out of curiosity and for amusement. He treats earth as an object of observation and calls it his hobby. His attitude toward humans is playful, superior, and somewhat mocking. He finds them emotional, limited, and less developed.

7) How do Roger Spelding and General Powers react differently to Kreton?

Answer

Roger Spelding reacts with curiosity and also sees the situation in terms of publicity and public attention. General Powers reacts with suspicion, seriousness, and military concern. Spelding is media-minded, while General Powers is authority-centered and defensive. Their responses reflect different aspects of human society.

8) What is Kreton’s first impression of earth people?

Answer

Kreton’s first impression of earth people is that they are foolish, emotional, proud, and less advanced. He finds them interesting but limited in understanding. His attitude creates humor and exposes human weaknesses.

9) What does the drama suggest about human pride, fear, and power?

Answer

The drama suggests that human pride is often exaggerated, fear makes people irrational, and power is frequently misused or misunderstood. Through Kreton’s presence, the play satirizes human institutions and shows how weak people become when faced with something beyond their control.

10) Why is Ellen attracted to Kreton’s strange personality?

Answer

Ellen is attracted to Kreton because he is mysterious, unusual, and different from ordinary people around her. Her curious and lively nature makes her interested in the unknown. Kreton’s confidence and strange powers make him fascinating to her.

SECTION B — LONG QUESTIONS WITH SOLUTIONS

Questions

1) Write a summary of “Self-Reliance.”

Answer

In “Self-Reliance,” Emerson teaches that the secret of greatness lies in trusting oneself. A person should not depend blindly on society or imitate others. True wisdom comes from inner confidence, independent thinking, and faith in one’s own abilities. Emerson says that envy is ignorance and imitation is suicide because they destroy individuality. A person should act according to conscience and remain firm in principles. He believes that peace and happiness cannot be achieved through outside approval, but through self-trust and self-respect. The lesson encourages courage, originality, and independence in thought and action.

2) Write a critical appreciation of “Sur Khahori.”

Answer

“Sur Khahori” is a deeply spiritual poem by Shah Abdul Latif Bhitai. It presents the wandering ascetic as a symbol of the human search for truth and union with the Beloved. The poem emphasizes patience, suffering, endurance, and devotion. The Khahori leaves comfort behind and travels through hardship in order to gain spiritual understanding. The language is simple but rich in imagery and symbolic meaning. The poem creates a serious and mystical tone. It teaches that real success lies not in worldly comfort but in spiritual realization achieved through sacrifice and discipline.

3) Discuss the theme, irony, and moral of “The Necklace.”

Answer

“The Necklace” is a story about vanity, false pride, and the difference between appearance and reality. Mathilde dreams of wealth and social status, and this desire leads her into misery. The central irony of the story is that she and her husband spend ten years in poverty to replace a necklace that was actually fake. This irony exposes the foolishness of valuing show over truth. The story teaches that greed, dissatisfaction, and love of luxury can ruin life. It also shows that honesty and contentment are far more valuable than social display.

4) Describe the struggle of Booker T. Washington to get education and the lessons we learn from it.

Answer

Booker T. Washington had a strong desire for education from an early age. When he heard about Hampton Institute, he became determined to go there. He faced severe poverty, lack of food, fatigue, and social hardship during the journey. At one point, he had to sleep under a sidewalk because he had nowhere to stay. Still, he did not lose hope. He worked hard, remained disciplined, and impressed others through his honesty and cleanliness. His struggle teaches us that success comes through determination, patience, hard work, and dedication to a noble goal.

5) Explain Quaid-e-Azam’s ideas about citizenship and nation-building.

Answer

Quaid-e-Azam believed that a successful nation must be built on justice, equality, discipline, and honesty. He said that all citizens of Pakistan were equal and should enjoy the freedom to practice their religion. He strongly condemned corruption, bribery, black-marketing, and nepotism because these evils weaken a country. He also emphasized law and order as necessary for peace and progress. His idea of nation-building was based on unity, fair treatment, and service to the common people. His message remains highly relevant even today.

6) Discuss the central ideas of “Choosing Career” and explain how a student should make a career choice.

Answer

“Choosing Career” explains that a career is not just a job but a long-term path that shapes one’s future. The lesson advises students to select a career according to their interests, skills, values, personality, and aptitude. It warns against choosing a profession only because of pressure from family or society. Students should understand their strengths and weaknesses, seek guidance from counsellors or experienced people, and make realistic plans. A wise career decision leads to confidence, satisfaction, and success in life.

7) Appreciate “Ozymandias” as a poem about pride and the fall of power.

Answer

“Ozymandias” is a powerful poem about human pride and the temporary nature of worldly power. It describes the ruins of a great king’s statue lying broken in the desert. The inscription proudly challenges others to look at his greatness, but there is nothing left except emptiness and decay. This contrast creates strong irony. The poem shows that rulers, kingdoms, and material achievements do not last forever. Through vivid imagery and irony, Shelley teaches that arrogance is meaningless before the power of time.

8) Discuss Kreton as the most important character in “A Visit to a Small Planet.”

Answer

Kreton is the central and most important character in the drama because the entire action revolves around his arrival and behavior. He is an alien with superior knowledge and unusual powers. Through him, the playwright satirizes human pride, fear, politics, and media culture. Kreton is curious, playful, confident, and often mocking toward humans. His presence exposes the weaknesses of earth people and creates both humor and tension. He is important not only as a character but also as a dramatic device through which the themes of the play are expressed.

GRAMMAR — SOLVED MODEL QUESTIONS

Questions

1) Change the narration:

He said, “I am tired.”

Answer

He said that he was tired.

2) Change the narration:

The teacher said to the students, “Work hard.”

Answer

The teacher advised the students to work hard.

3) Change the voice:

Ali wrote a letter.

Answer

A letter was written by Ali.

4) Change the voice:

They will complete the work tomorrow.

Answer

The work will be completed tomorrow.

5) Fill in the blank with correct preposition:

He is interested ___ poetry.

Answer

in

6) Fill in the blank with correct article:

He is ___ honest man.

Answer

an

7) Correct the sentence:

She do not likes tea.

Answer

She does not like tea.

8) Correct the sentence:

Each of the boys have done their work.

Answer

Each of the boys has done his work.

9) Change into compound sentence:

Being tired, he went to bed.

Answer

He was tired, so he went to bed.

10) Change into complex sentence:

He worked hard to succeed.

Answer

He worked hard so that he might succeed.

11) Remove “too”:

He is too weak to walk.

Answer

He is so weak that he cannot walk.

12) Remove “as soon as”:

As soon as he saw me, he smiled.

Answer

No sooner did he see me than he smiled.

13) Change into positive degree:

Aslam is stronger than any other boy in the class.

Answer

No other boy in the class is as strong as Aslam.

14) Use correct form of verb:

If I ___ rich, I would help the poor.

Answer

were

15) Use correct pronoun:

Everyone should do ___ duty.

Answer

his
